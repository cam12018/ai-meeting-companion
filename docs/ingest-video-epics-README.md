In one or two sentences, what's your big idea?
We're building an AI-powered meeting companion that ingests audio from tools like Zoom, Microsoft Teams, and Slack Huddles and uses AWS services to automatically generate structured summaries, identify blockers, and turn action items into Slack updates or Jira tickets. The goal is to close the gap between conversation and execution while reducing meeting overhead.
Tell us about your vision. What exactly will you build?
We will build a multi-platform AI meeting companion that turns workplace conversations into execution-ready outcomes. The system ingests meeting audio from tools such as Zoom, Microsoft Teams, and Slack Huddles through a unified ingestion layer, transcribes conversations using Amazon Transcribe, and applies Amazon Bedrock to extract progress updates, blockers, decisions, and clearly defined action items. Before any updates are share or taks are created, users are presented with a review screen that lists all detected action items and proposed Slack notifications or Jira tickets, allowing teams to confirm, edit, or discard items. Once approved, the structured summaries and actions are delivered to the tools teams already use. The application is built entirely on serverless AWS services with event-driven workflows, ephemeral audio storage, and configurable usage limits to maintain privacy, scalability, and cost efficiency within AWS free-tier constraints.
How will your solution make a difference?
Our solution reduces the gap between conversation and execution by ensuring that important decisions and action items from meetings are never lost. Teams spend significant time in meetings, yet outcomes are often forgotten, inconsistently documented, or manually translated into follow-up work. By automatically transforming meeting audio into structured summaries and clearly defined action items, our solution helps teams stay aligned accountable, and focused on execution. The solution benefits remote and hybrid teams, engineering and product organizations, and small to mid-sized companies that rely heavily on collaboration tools such as Zoom, Microsoft Teams, and Slack. Team leads and managers gain better visibility into progress and blockers, while individual contributors are freed from manual documentation and follow-up tasks. Organizations with limited operation resources benefit from a lightweight, automated approach that integrates directly into existing workflows.
What's your game plan for building this?
We will build this solution in steps using AWS serverless services to ensure rapid development, low cost, and strong privacy guarantees. A Zoom webhook will notify our system when a meeting recording is available. An AWS Lambda function, exposed through API Gateway, will securely retrieve the recording and process it in an ephemeral workflow. Audio is deleted immediately after processing the transcription. Amazon Transcribe converts the audio into text, after which the transcript is passed to Amazon Bedrock to generate structured outputs including standup summaries, blockers, and action items with owners. Only the final AI-generated summaries and minimal metadata are stored for reference and auditing. When processing completes, a Slack message notifies the team that insights are ready and includes a secure, time-limited review link. This link opens a lightweight static review page hosted on Amazon S3, backed by serverless APIs using API Gateway and Lambda. Users can validate and edit summaries, approve action items, and confirm which Jira tickets or Slack updates should be created. Upon approval, AWS Lambda orchestrates downstream integrations by posting formatted summaries to Slack via webhooks and creating Jira tickets through their REST APIs, using securely stored credentials. The ingestion pipeline is platform-agnostic and designed to support additional tools such as Teams and Huddles. All infrastructure scales to zero and operates within AWS Free Tier limits.
